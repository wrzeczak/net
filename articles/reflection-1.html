Reflection no.1
Written 21 November 2024.

Return to All Articles

How much of a fool was I to not recognize unconditional love? To fail to recognize myself, as someone unloveable, who yet still was; someone who was yet still loved by someone more beautiful than him, by someone entirely beautiful!

"How is reality? How has it pleased you?" Has living in the face of love I rejected with no basis (as if there could be such a basis! For spirit to reject an answer is unthinkable.) pleased me, or has it brought me to profane realizations, profane ideology, i.e. to the bullshit I spew everyday. What has it brought me but disgust? It is always an awful moment when something unloved intrudes upon something loved.

My only prayer is that I did not kill her like Yasuko, and perhaps Kyoko, were so brutally slain. This is the only thing that I can motivate myself to prayer with, by my faith in sensuality.

Was a fool I was — "all we did was fuck" —  as if I should have wished for love without lust? It is as if I had wished to live life permanently choking.

I think I loved her like I only should have loved a man; with camraderie, with beauty, carnality. My confusion was through these crossed wires. I look at every other woman I "love" and I wish with all my heart that she were a man. She was my only friend, and that was among her greatest strengths, in her infinite power. That I could love her as Socrates loved his boyfriends, and his friends (that is, one and the same) was truly remarkable. It was remarkable that I could know her in lust. It was remarkable that she did this in the fullness of her femininity, and that by my love for her in a masculine context, she extended herself to the fullest reaches of humanity. She was more human than I, living in the breadth of masculinity and femininity.

Even if it were true that she did not know me, what should it have mattered? Do I even love me? I can barely say I do. In her beauty, vitality, in all her strength, she could do both.
